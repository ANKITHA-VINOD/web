Expectations, Lockdown and Realisations

I have always thought that some time off from all the chaos would give me peace , a kind of stability. I thought keeping away from humans would bring calmness. Hence the idea of a lockdown was initially an interesting one for me. I dreamt of myself being productive and "living life to the fullest potential". As obvious as it is, I over expected and the master- procrastinator in me came out.

The initial few days, I chose to relax. I woke up late in the afternoon because I went to sleep early in the morning. Something called "Brunch" came to my life (It's like a combo of breakfast and lunch ). Then social media took over. I found myself jumping and drifting between WhatsApp , Facebook, Instagram and even Twitter( No I don't have a Twitter account). Thanks to the many strangers that I am following in these platforms, my feed always seems to be brimming with posts and articles I find no serious connections with. 

Everyone had already started putting throwback posts and I was wondering what I should post so as to " Make people think I had a wonderful life back then and that I enjoyed life back then even though in reality I didn't but I still pretend because it's mandatory and make others realise that How bad a life they had simply because they didn't post any TB posts" . It's that simple.

And just then I noticed that the trend had shifted in a matter of days- It's digital painting that's finding place. And it seemed like everyone's cup of tea. So I too started one just to realise later that I needed a cup of coffee and not tea.And all those posts commanding to make this lockdown "Productive" scared the hell out of me. Am I just wasting that once in a century/lifetime "Golden-Diamond" chance to be productive and realise the real me?Last but not the least, I would also like to thank my Whatsapp uncles and aunties for keeping me updated with the latest fake news.Now all I know is that The lockdown is getting longer, My dark circles getting deeper, My weight getting higher.

But I still choose to be happy because My tummy has realised it's potential- it's growth is indeed impressive!
